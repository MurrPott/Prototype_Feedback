What is carbon footprint?

A carbon footprint is the total amount of greenhouse gases, like carbon dioxide, we create through our everyday actions. This includes things like driving cars, using electricity, or making products. These gases trap heat in the atmosphere and contribute to climate change.

By understanding our carbon footprint, we can find ways to reduce it—like using less energy or switching to greener choices—to help protect the planet for future generations. It’s all about making smarter, eco-friendly decisions!

How YOU can reduce your carbon footprint

You can reduce your carbon footprint by making simple, everyday changes.

Use less energy by turning off lights and unplugging devices when not in use. Walk, bike, or use public transport instead of driving. Switch to renewable energy or energy-efficient appliances if you can. Eat more plant-based foods and waste less. Recycle and reuse items to cut down on waste.

Small steps like these help lower the greenhouse gases we create and make a big difference for the planet!
